Here is the updated summary, including the specific encryption technologies used to secure the app:

### Core Concept
SecureVault is a modern, personal password manager that allows users to securely store and organize all their sensitive information—like website logins, door codes, and private notes—and keep it synced across all their devices.

### High-Level Flow
When you open the app, you sign in and unlock your personal "vault" using a unique master password. Once inside, you can view a clean list of your saved passwords, tap one to copy it, or click a button to add a new secret. Behind the scenes, whenever you save a new password, the app scrambles (encrypts) the information directly on your phone or computer before sending it to the cloud. This means by the time your data is backed up, it is completely unreadable to anyone without your master password.

### Technologies Used

**Frontend (What the user sees and interacts with)**
*   **React:** A popular tool created by Facebook for building fast and interactive websites. It is used here because it makes the app feel snappy and instantly updates what you see on the screen without reloading the page.
*   **Tailwind CSS & Radix UI:** Tools used to design and style the app. They make it easy to create a beautiful, modern-looking interface (like buttons and menus) that works well on both mobile screens and desktop monitors.

**Backend & Database (Where data is stored)**
*   **Google Firebase (Firestore):** A cloud database service that stores your information. It was chosen because it excels at "real-time syncing," ensuring that if you add a password on your computer, it instantly shows up on your phone.

**Authentication & Security (How users log in & measures to protect data)**
*   **Firebase Authentication:** A secure system that manages user accounts. It provides a safe way for users to sign up using an email and password, or quickly log in using their existing Google account, without the developers having to build a login system from scratch.
*   **Web Crypto API:** The built-in browser technology used to handle heavy-duty security tasks directly on your device, ensuring speed and reliability without needing external plugins.
*   **PBKDF2 (Password-Based Key Derivation Function 2):** A security algorithm used to take your human-readable master password and stretch it out (doing the math 600,000 times) to create an incredibly strong, unguessable digital "key." This protects against hackers trying to guess your password quickly.
*   **AES-GCM (256-bit) Encryption:** The gold standard for data encryption (the same standard used by banks and the military). It takes your actual passwords and scrambles them into unbreakable gibberish *before* they ever leave your device. The "GCM" part also acts like a tamper-evident seal, ensuring no one has secretly altered your data while it was stored in the cloud.

**Other Important Libraries & Services**
*   **Capacitor:** A clever tool that takes this web-based application and wraps it up so that it can be downloaded and installed as a "real" mobile app on Android or Apple iPhones.
*   **Framer Motion:** A library used to add smooth, fluid animations to the interface, making the app feel premium and polished when you click around.
