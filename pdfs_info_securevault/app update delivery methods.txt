That is a great question. Since you are using **Capacitor** with React/Vite, you have a massive advantage: your app consists of a "Native Shell" (the APK part) and a "Web Bundle" (your HTML, CSS, JS). 

For 95% of your updates (UI changes, new features, bug fixes), you **only need to update the Web Bundle**. You only need a new APK if you add a new native plugin (like a barcode scanner or native biometrics).

Here is the best, near-zero-cost workflow to deliver updates instantly without forcing users to download and install a new APK every time.

---

### Route 1: Over-The-Air (OTA) Updates via Capgo (Highly Recommended)
[Capgo](https://capgo.app/) is the modern standard for Capacitor OTA updates. It allows you to push updates directly to your users' devices in the background.

**How it works:**
1. You build your app (`npm run build`).
2. You push the update using the Capgo CLI to their servers.
3. When your users open the app, it downloads the update in the background.
4. The next time they open the app, it instantly swaps to the new version.

**Cost:** They have a very generous free tier (perfect for starting out) and open-source self-hosting options if you want to run it completely free on a cheap VPS later.

**Setup Workflow:**
1. Install the plugin: `npm i @capacitor-updater/core`
2. Sync capacitor: `npx cap sync`
3. Add a few lines of code to your `index.tsx` or `App.tsx` to tell the app to check for updates on startup.
4. To release an update, you just type: `npx capgo upload` in your terminal.

---

### Route 2: The Progressive Web App (PWA) Route (100% Free, No APK Needed)
Since you are bypassing the Play Store entirely, you might want to consider skipping the APK distribution altogether. Capacitor apps are just web apps at their core.

**How it works:**
1. You deploy your app to **Firebase Hosting** (which is free/extremely cheap).
2. Users visit your app's URL on their phone's browser (Chrome/Safari).
3. They get a prompt to **"Add to Home Screen"**.
4. The app installs to their phone, gets an app icon, and opens fullscreen exactly like a native app.

**Why this is perfect for your situation:**
* **Zero Cost:** Firebase Hosting is essentially free for small/medium user bases.
* **Instant Updates:** The moment you run `firebase deploy`, every user gets the new version the next time they open the app. No update servers, no background downloads required.
* **Safer Distribution:** You don't have to convince users to bypass Android's scary "Install Unknown Apps" security warnings to sideload your APK.

### Route 3: In-App "Update Available" Prompt (Manual APK download)
If you strictly want to stick to distributing APKs but want to make it easier for users to know there's an update:
1. Store a "latest_version" document in your Firestore database or a static `version.json` file on Firebase Hosting.
2. When the app starts, it fetches this file and compares it to its current version.
3. If a newer version exists, show a nice popup inside your app: *"A new update is available! [Download Now]"*.
4. The button links directly to your server or a Google Drive link where the new `.apk` is hosted.

**Cost:** 100% Free.
**Downside:** The user still has to click download and go through the Android install prompt, but at least they are notified immediately inside the app.

---

**My Recommendation:**  
If your app absolutely needs native features (like native FaceID/Biometrics), go with **Route 1 (Capgo)**. It gives you the "magic" update experience where the app just fixes itself silently. 

If your app doesn't rely heavily on low-level native hardware features, definitely go with **Route 2 (PWA)**. It is the easiest, cheapest, and most friction-free way to distribute a web-based app outside of the App Stores.
