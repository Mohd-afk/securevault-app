Installing **all** MCP servers from that list is **not recommended** for several reasons:

1. **Resource usage** – Each MCP server runs as a background process. Too many can slow down your editor/IDE.
2. **Tool conflicts** – Some servers may expose similarly named tools, causing ambiguity or errors.
3. **Context overload** – The AI will have access to dozens of tools. Without clear instructions, it may pick the wrong one or waste tokens deciding.
4. **Security** – Granting access to many services (databases, cloud, payment) increases risk if misused.

The AI/agent **does not automatically use** any server just because it's installed. You must either:
- **Explicitly ask** the AI to use a specific tool (e.g., "Check my Firestore data").
- **Configure** the AI to have certain servers enabled for a task.
- Some advanced setups let the AI decide based on context, but that's still limited and may need fine-tuning.

## ✅ **How to Choose**

- Start with **only the servers you actively need** for your current task.
- Add more as you require them.
- Keep your workspace lean to avoid confusion.

If you're building a password manager, I'd begin with **Firebase** (for auth & database) and **GitHub** (for code). Add others later as needed.
